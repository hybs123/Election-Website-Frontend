LATEST DEBATES ON ELECTION 2024

The political landscape is changing ahead of the 2024 Lok Sabha Elections, as leaders are changing alliances and parties. The importance of ideological orientation is growing, according to political scientist Praveen Rai, who divides it into three categories: left, right, and centrist. He observes that it affects how people vote. Another expert, Chandan Gowda, believes that ideology is significant but not crucial. He stresses the importance of having an inspiring vision that the public can relate to. The shifting coalitions and ideological factors add a dynamic element to the Congress and INDIA bloc's efforts to oppose the BJP, hence influencing the political environment.

Lok Sabha 2024 a 'battle of ideologies': Congress could be a game-changer if...

The Congress has repeatedly emphasised that this year's Lok Sabha Elections will be a "battle of ideologies".

At the India Today Conclave Mumbai 2023, political leaders from the NDA and the Opposition's INDIA bloc engaged in a debate over their prospects in the upcoming general elections. The panel on Day 2 included Shiv Sena (Uddhav Balasaheb Thackeray) MP Priyanka Chaturvedi, Shiv Sena MP Rahul Shewale, BJP leader Gaurav Bhatia, and Congress leader Supriya Shrinate. Each representative presented their arguments on why their respective alliances would emerge victorious in the 2024 general elections, highlighting the intensity of the political discourse and the anticipation surrounding the electoral outcomes.

In order to appeal to voters and prevent a rise in the fiscal deficit, Prime Minister Narendra Modi plans to use India's final budget before the elections as leverage. The strong economy has brought in a windfall for taxes, which has helped the government reach its deficit reduction goals. In her sixth budget statement on February 1, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman is predicted to maintain infrastructure expenditure and roll out specific projects for Modi's main political constituencies, which are women, young people, farmers, and the impoverished. Economists predict a modest budget despite the economic growth, viewing it as a stopgap measure until the next administration takes office.
